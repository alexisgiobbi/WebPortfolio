Sound editing is a little out of my comfort zone, but this semester I became proficient in the sound-editing software Audacity. I used the software to create a narrative soundscape that tells a ~spooky~ story. I used basic tools like splitting, cutting, and joining, and also more advanced tools like distortion, pitching, and ducking. I'm definitely open to experiment with sound design and editing in the future, if the occassion ever arises

The narration was done by myself and a friend, and the sound effects are a mix of original free-to-use sounds. The soundscape is embedded here, but to get the orginal file or see more of the process, click here
